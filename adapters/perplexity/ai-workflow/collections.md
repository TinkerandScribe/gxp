# Suggested Perplexity Collections for GXP

Perplexity Collections are a powerful way to organize ongoing research. This document suggests a useful default set of Collections for someone using the GXP workflow regularly.

## Recommended Core Collections

### 1. Technology Radar
**Purpose:** Track technologies you're evaluating or might use in the future.

**Suggested Threads:**
- Language / Runtime evaluations
- Framework comparisons
- Infrastructure & DevOps tools
- Data / Storage technologies
- AI/ML tooling

**Benefit:** Reduces repeated research and helps you maintain a living view of the technology landscape.

### 2. Architecture Patterns
**Purpose:** Capture high-quality architectural knowledge and trade-off analysis.

**Suggested Threads:**
- Distributed systems patterns
- Real-time / collaboration systems
- Authentication & authorization approaches
- Scalability and performance patterns
- Testing and observability strategies

### 3. Competitive & Market Intelligence
**Purpose:** Stay aware of what others in your space are doing.

**Suggested Threads:**
- Competitor product & technical analysis
- Industry trends (e.g., "AI tooling landscape 2025")
- Build vs buy decisions in your domain

### 4. Failure Modes & War Stories
**Purpose:** Learn from other people's expensive mistakes.

**Suggested Threads:**
- Post-mortems and incident reports
- "Why [popular project] failed" style research
- Common anti-patterns in [your domain]

This collection often produces extremely high-value input for the **Out of Scope** and **failure capture** parts of GXP briefs.

### 5. Domain / Business Context (Optional but Powerful)
If you work in a specific domain (fintech, healthcare, developer tools, etc.), maintain a Collection for that domain's unique constraints, regulations, and best practices.

## Usage Tips

- Keep Collections focused. It's better to have 5–8 well-curated Collections than 30 noisy ones.
- Periodically review older threads in your Collections during Phase 0 of new GXP tasks.
- When you do significant research, consider saving the best threads into the relevant Collection for future you (or future team members).

## Relationship to GXP

Good Collections act as a **personal knowledge base** that makes your Phase 0 research dramatically faster and higher quality over time. They are one of the highest-leverage ways to compound the benefits of using Perplexity inside the GXP workflow.
